Privacy first: you can prove to vendor A that you have an active subscription with vendor B without leaking and personal information.

Anti-piracy: you have to share your private key to share your license, as the token is transferrable you risk it being transferred away from you.

Step 1: Buy a token

After clicking the buy button metamask should open and ask you to make a transaction. This is on testnet so not real ether.

Step 2: Check your balance

It may take a minute for your transaction to be mined. Make sure you have a balance of 1 or more before proceeding.

Step 3: Securely prove you own your etherum address

A web server sends you a challenge message that you sign with metamask. This signature proves to the server that you own the ethereum address.

OpenSign

OpenSign is a decentralized alternative to products like DocuSign and EchoSign. Documents are stored on IPFS, a decentralized filesystem, and the signatures are stored on the Ethereum blockchain. The only thing that's left is the code that's running in your browser right now, and that's hosted on IPFS too!
